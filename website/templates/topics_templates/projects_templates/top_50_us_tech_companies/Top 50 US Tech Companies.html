Initial EDA

Since we have no null values, we do not need to drop any columns or deal with that isue. Stock Name/Industry/Company Name are distinct features, so we cannot derive any meaningful information, but we can use them to represent our datapoints.

We can perform EDA based on Sector/State and find patterns for revenue, market cap, annual income and employee size and we can also try to find patterns between the continuous variables of our dataset.

Sector Analysis

In [15]:
cols = [
    "Annual Revenue 2022-2023 (USD in Billions)",
    "Annual Income Tax in 2022-2023 (USD in Billions)",
    "Employee Size",
    "Market Cap (USD in Trillions)",
]

for col in cols:
    new_df = pd.merge(
        (df[["Sector", col]].groupby("Sector").sum()),
        (df[["Sector", col]].groupby("Sector").mean()),
        left_index=True,
        right_index=True,
        suffixes=(" Total", " Mean"),
    )
    fig = new_df.plot(kind="bar")

    fig.update_layout(title={"text": f"{col} per Sector"})
    fig.update_layout(barmode="group")
    fig.show()
In [16]:
for col in cols:
    percent = df[["Sector", col]].groupby("Sector").sum()
    percent[f"{col} %"] = round(100 * (percent[col] / percent[col].sum()), 2)

    fig = px.pie(names=percent.index, values=percent[f"{col} %"])
    fig.update_traces(textinfo="label+percent", title=f"Sector Breakdown of {col}")
    fig.show()

We can see that most market capitalisation, and the biggest companies in general, are focused arround Software application and infrastracture, with the outlier of Consumer Electronics, which while having 20% of the total market cap, they only correspond to 3.94% of the total number of employees.

We can see that the market cap closely correlates with the annual revenue and the annual income tax. It is surprising however, that the employee size is not related to the size of the company in terms of tax and market cap, which could be anticipated by the example we gave earlier about Consumer Electronics.
